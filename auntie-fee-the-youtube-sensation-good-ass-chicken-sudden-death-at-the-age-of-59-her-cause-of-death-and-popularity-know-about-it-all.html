Auntie Fee the YouTube Sensation good ass chicken, sudden Death at the age of 59!!! Her Cause

Updated On December 1, 2023 | Published By: prisma pulitzer | In Career , Death

Auntie Fee, the YouTube Sensation “good ass chicken,” expired at the age of 59.

The sad news has taken all the fans to mourn her death. Auntie Fee, the viral video sensation who is also known as Chef Sista Girl, has died nearly a week after suffering a massive heart attack … according to her son.

The Sudden Death

Fee’s son Tavis Hunter announced her death via his social media handle, Facebook. He posted late at night,

“god made the decision to take my mother home where its peace & Joy and I’m okay with that.”

Tavis, Fee’s son, is the channel’s cameraman too.

He wrote,

“Thank you for all the prayers and hope, It did all it can do and now god made the decision to take my mother home where its peace & Joy and I’m okay with that, She can finally Be happy. Rip Momma Felicia Auntie Fee O’Dell I love you and you was my twin, When u was going through it, i went through it, and now its time for you to relax and watch me do it baby.”

Fee’s real name is Felicia O’Dell. She was placed on life support after suffering from a massive heart attack on Tuesday, according to TMZ.

She was taken to Harbor-UCLA Medical Center. Fee became an internet sensation in 2014 for her unique cooking videos on YouTube. She quickly became known for her “good ass chicken” and “sweet treats for the kids” videos.

Fame of Felicia O’Dell

The fee came along with fame some people criticized her. Some called her illiterate, unsanitary, and unscripted. She was called a bad example for other black folks. Critics also noted that the dishes she cooked were loaded with butter, sugar, and grease and would send one to an early grave. However, the criticism didn’t matter, the cause wouldn’t and didn’t change.

In an interview with a Times article in 2015, she said,

“I’m gonna keep it 100 and be me. Ain’t nobody gonna tell me I gotta do this, I gotta do that,”

“If you don’t have crescent rolls, you can use any kind of cheap a– donuts or whatever, it doesn’t matter. I don’t know what the f*ck this is, it’s just something sweet for the f*cking kids.”

One of her most viewed videos, which currently has more than 2 million views, is about feeding a family of seven with just $3.35.
