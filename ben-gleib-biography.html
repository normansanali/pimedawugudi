Who is Ben Gleib?

Ben Gleib is a famous American actor, comedian, satirist, and writer.

He is known as the “Esquire” of comedy and one of the best six comedians in America. He became popular through his comical style.

Ben Gleib: Professional Life, Career

Gleib started his acting career in 1998, playing the role of a homeless kid in the romantic comedy film To Hell with Love. He wrote “Radio Music Awards” for ABC in 2002.

Then he directed, wrote, and starred in three seasons on the National Lampoon Network for his own show from 2003 to 2005. He released his first stand-up comedy album, Ben Gleib, in 2007.

He made his TV debut on NBC’s hidden-camera show, The Real Wedding Crashers, in which comedians teamed up with a couple to prank unsuspecting guests at their wedding.

Then, he appeared on the late-night talk show Chelsea Lately in January 2008.

He has worked in many shows, TV series, and films. In 2012 he gave his voice to the role of Marshall in Ice Age:  Continental Drift. In addition, he worked with many TV series, Shows, short movies, and films.

Gleib launched his campaign for President of the United States on Twitter on May 13, 2019. Gleib’s campaign finished on December 30, 2019.
