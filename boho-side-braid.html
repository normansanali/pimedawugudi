10 Side Braids That Are Totally DIY-able

When it comes to updos, we love the simplicity of a ponytail and the elegance of a bun, but there's something to be said of the statement side braid. While previous iterations favored complicated fishtail styles, today's version is much more nuanced. So regardless of your braiding skills (or lack thereof), you're bound to find a side braid that works for you. In fact, we rounded up 10 of the most swoon-worthy plaited looks as worn by our favorite A-listers. From bold statement-making braids to barely-there braided accents, these side braid styles are sure to inspire.

Keep scrolling for 10 of our favorite side braids to try out ASAP.

Boho Chic

Elsa is that you? We had to do a double take when Cara Delevingne showed up to Paris Fashion Week with this Frozen-esque side braid. Chic side-swept bangs were the perfect counterpart to her loose, undone, plait.

Braids on Braids

Storm Reid went all out with her braided look at Milan Fashion Week. Her cornrows gave way to a lengthy side braid that was tight and polished (read: no flyaways here). As for those baby hairs? They were laid down to perfection.

Bubble-icious

Not convinced of a traditional side braid? Follow Issa Rae's lead and go for a bubble version instead. To play up the glam factor (and Y2K effect), add some hair gems to your crown.

"I Woke Up Like This"

Heidi Klum proves that a mussy side braid — although seemingly casual — can be dressed up for any occasion. To get her look, loosely braid your hair and place it over one shoulder. Then, pull out a few pieces before finishing with a douse of texturizing spray. The result will be visually effortless.

Teeny Tiny Accent

Side braids come in all shapes and sizes. Take Arden Cho's teeny tiny version, for instance. It's a great option if you don't want to go full-on side braid but want a little somethin' somethin' to spruce up your style. It also works if you've got shorter strands, too.

Ponytail Hybrid

Can't decide between a braid or a ponytail? Then why not try both? This hybrid hairstyle, as modeled on Melissa Barrera, is visually impressive — especially with a lengthy mane — but a breeze to pull off.

The Mashup

At the Spirit Awards, Kerry Washington turned up to the red carpet with this eclectic multi-braided 'do — and we loved all of it. Rather than go with a standard three-plaited braid, she opted for a two-plaited rope side braid instead.

Oh-So-Dreamy

Camila Cabello is #hairgoals. Here, her lengthy, textured side braid with wispy bangs and fallen strands is utterly romantic. While this style works best on those with long, thick hair but it can also be adapted to fit any hair type and texture.

Accessorized to the Max

Sometimes a simple side braid is not enough. Janelle Monae chose to complement hers with whimsical white puffs and a hat, but you can also wear it with a headband, bows, or your hair accessory(ies) of choice.

Double the Fun

Why wear one braid when you can wear two? Amandla Stenberg's dual side braids combined with a stark middle part ooze stylish sophistication. To keep with the sleek aesthetic, we recommend tying your braid with a clear elastic (e.g. Goody Ouchless Hair Ties).
