Biography of Charles Leclerc

In October 1997, Charles Leclerc was born in Monte Carlo, Monaco. He began his karting career in 2005, and in that year, as well as in 2006 and 2008, he won the French PACA Championship.

He debuted in Formula One with Sauber in 2018. Leclerc raced for Ferrari in the #16 car in 2019. He finished in 13th place in 2018 and won the Belgian and Italian Grand Prix races in 2019. In 2017, Charles Leclerc debuted in the FIA Formula 2 Championship.

In 2016 and 2017, he served as a test driver for Formula One. In 2016 for ART Grand Prix, Leclerc won the GP3 Series, and in 2017 for Prema Racing, he won the FIA Formula 2 Championship. He was close friends with the late Jules Bianchi, who served as Leclerc’s godfather, and the two were like family.

Since Olivier Beretta in 1994, he was the first Monegasque driver in the Formula One.

Ferrari bought Leclerc in 2019 to take Kimi Raikkonen’s seat.

Leclerc received a brand-new contract at the conclusion of his rookie campaign, and he is now committed to Ferrari through the end of the 2024 season.

His estimated annual wage is £9.8 million ($12 million), which is comparable to that of his colleague Carlos Sainz but significantly less than the around £40 million that Mercedes’ Lewis Hamilton and Red Bull’s Max Verstappen take home each year.

Who are the parents of Charles Leclerc? Charles Leclerc was born to Hervé Leclerc and Pascale Leclerc.

Hervé was also a driver in Formula 3 from 1980s to 1990s. He passed away after a battle with a long complication at age 54 just four days before Leclerc won the feature at the 2017 Baku Formula 2 round.
