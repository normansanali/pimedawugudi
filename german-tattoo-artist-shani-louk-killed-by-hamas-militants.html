German tattoo artist Shani Louk killed by Hamas militants

During the attack, Shani Louk was taken by Hamas militants and later found dead.

Disturbing footage emerged showing her lifeless body being paraded on the back of a pick-up truck by militants who claimed she was an Israeli soldier.

The footage showed militants jeering and spitting on her body. Shani’s cousin Tomasina Weintraub-Louk confirmed her identity due to distinctive leg tattoos and dreadlocks.

Shani’s family has been anxiously awaiting news about her and is devastated by the situation.

Her mother, Ricarda, shared an emotional plea for help, explaining that Shani was kidnapped by Palestinian Hamas militants along with a group of tourists.

Another woman, Noa Argamani, was also reportedly kidnapped from the same festival by Hamas militants.

She was seen pleading for her life as she was driven away on a motorbike. Later, photos appeared to show Noa alive in Hamas custody in Gaza.

British national Jake Marlowe, who was working as security for the music festival, is also missing.

He sent a last message to his mother, expressing concern about rockets flying overhead.
