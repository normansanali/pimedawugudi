Romeo and Duet viewers cringe as Sheffield woman appears on ITV's 'worst ever' dating show

A Sheffield woman was one of Saturday night’s contestants on ITV show, Romeo and Duet, dubbed the “worst show ever” by viewers.

Viewers of the show have taken to Twitter this evening naming the “bad” programme their “guilty pressure”. Selina, from Sheffield, was one of tonight’s contestants.

She appeared fussy as she failed to walk down from the balcony as the love-match attempted to serenade her. She said: “I wanted to cut his hair”, when seeing the match for the first time.

Read more: Gogglebox stars Giles and Mary’s nerves are shaken by ‘The Freaks’ on Britain’s Got Talent

The show sees single love seekers stand on a balcony where they are then serenaded by love matches they can hear but can’t see. Singers have just one song to entice the potential love-match down from the balcony before coming face-to-face for the first time.

If a match, the newly formed couple will then head off on a duet-date to learn a duet, before returning later to perform that number in the ultimate romantic love battle. The crowd’s chosen couple will then get whisked off for another date to see if love can truly blossom.

Selina chose the second person who appeared on the show to go on a duet-date with. Viewers shared their views on Twitter, saying the show is “ridiculous”.

Becca said: “It’s so so bad, I can’t stop watching tho!!!”. Hannah said: “It’s ridiculous but it might be a guilty pleasure.”

Another viewer added: “Romeo and Duet is one of the worst shows ever commissioned for television.”

Paul said: “It’s truly unbelievable that they have even come up with this sh***, let alone it actually making the TV. What’s even worse is it’s on in my house.”

A teacher, Jessica from Castleford, also appeared on the show in an attempt to serenade a love-match.
