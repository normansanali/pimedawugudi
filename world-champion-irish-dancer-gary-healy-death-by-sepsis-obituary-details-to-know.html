Sepsis is a condition that develops unpredictably and can move quite quickly. When the condition is very severe, one or more of the body’s organ systems may fail.

In the most severe situations, the patient’s blood pressure decreases, the patient’s heart begins to weaken, and the patient begins to spiral toward septic shock.
